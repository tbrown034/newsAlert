## Pricing Models

### Model A: Tool-First (Conservative)

| Tier | Price | Features |
|------|-------|----------|
| Free | $0 | Basic feed, Haiku summaries, 24hr history |
| Pro | $4/mo | Opus analysis, full history, no rate limits |

**Ceiling**: ~$4-5/mo competing with free alternatives
**Expected conversion**: 1-3% of free users

---

### Model B: Editorial-First (Higher Value)

| Tier | Price | Features |
|------|-------|----------|
| Free | $0 | Basic feed, delayed briefings |
| Subscriber | $7/mo | Real-time Opus + editorial takes during breaking events |
| Founding Member | $15/mo | Above + community access, input on coverage |

**Expected conversion**: 3-7% (personality-driven products convert better)

---

### Model C: Hybrid (Recommended)

| Tier | Price | Features |
|------|-------|----------|
| Free | $0 | Feed + Haiku summaries |
| Pulse Pro | $5/mo | Opus analysis, full features |
| Pulse Intel | $12/mo | Pro + editorial briefings during major events |

**Revenue projection** (1,000 users):
- 30 Pro subscribers (3%) = $150/mo
- 15 Intel subscribers (1.5%) = $180/mo
- **Total: $330/mo**

Scale to 10,000 users → $3,300/mo potential

## Alternative Monetization Methods

### Tier 1: Low Effort, Low Friction

#### Tip Jar / Donations
| Platform | Cut | Best For |
|----------|-----|----------|
| Buy Me a Coffee | 5% | One-time tips |
| Ko-fi | 0% | Tips + monthly supporters |
| GitHub Sponsors | 0% | Developer audience |

**Realistic revenue**: $50-500/mo with 1,000+ engaged users

#### Pay What You Want
Everything stays free, ask for voluntary support (Wikipedia model).
**Conversion**: 0.5-2% of active users

---

### Tier 2: Medium Effort, Sustainable Revenue

#### Journalism Grants & Foundation Funding

| Funder | Focus | Award Range |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Knight Foundation | News innovation | $10K-500K |
| Google News Initiative | Digital journalism | $5K-300K |
| Mozilla Foundation | Internet health | $10K-100K |
| Craig Newmark Philanthropies | Trustworthy journalism | Varies |
| Democracy Fund | Civic engagement | $25K-250K |
| Open Society Foundations | Independent media | Varies |

**Grant pitch angle**: "Open-source intelligence dashboard democratizing access to real-time global awareness, countering misinformation by aggregating verified sources."

**Realistic revenue**: $10K-100K grants achievable with working product
**Effort**: 5-20 hours per application, ~10-20% hit rate

#### API Access / Data Licensing

| Tier | Price | Audience |
|------|-------|----------|
| Hobbyist | $10/mo | Developers, researchers |
| Startup | $50/mo | Small apps, dashboards |
| Enterprise | $200+/mo | Newsrooms, analysts |

**What you're selling**: Pre-aggregated, cleaned, geo-tagged feed from 478 curated sources.

| Customer | Use Case | Price Range |
|----------|----------|-------------|

| Security firms | Internal monitoring | $500-2,000/mo |

#### Educational Products

#### Sponsored Deep Dives (Ethical Version)

**Ethical guidelines**:
- Foundation-sponsored coverage only
- Sponsor has zero editorial input
- Clearly disclosed
- Mission-aligned sponsors (foundations, universities — not defense contractors)

---

### Tier 4: Long-Shot, High Upside

#### Nonprofit Model (501c3)

Convert to nonprofit for:
- Foundation grant eligibility
- Tax-deductible donations
- Mission protection

**Examples**: ProPublica, The Marshall Project, Bellingcat

#### Acquisition Target

Potential buyers if Pulse gets traction:
- News organizations (tool + talent)
- Security/intel firms (tech + sources)
- Larger aggregators (user base)
